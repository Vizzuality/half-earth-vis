In the past half-century, species that were once virtually innumerable have practically disappeared from the Earth.

In fact the rate of extinction today is at least 1,000 times higher than at any time in Earth’s history.

Rhinos used to range widely through Sub-Saharan Africa and Southeast Asia.

But with much of its habitat replaced by agriculture, and its populations driven downward by relentless poachers, rhinos are now limited almost entirely to a few captives in zoos.

Human beings are connected with all of life. Over 3.8 billion years, the organisms that surround us have evolved to create a delicate balance of interconnected resilience.

We depend upon all of nature in the same way that nature depends upon us.

Suitable habitat, crucial to nature’s survival, is shrinking fast. We must act before we’ve lost irreplaceable species and ecosystems forever.
